## vs. Aggro (Red Deck Wins, White Weenie, Boros)

**Boarding:**
- **In (4):** 2x Bounce Off, 1x Authority of the Consuls, 1x Elspeth, Storm Slayer
- **Out (4):** 2x Negate, 1x Make Your Own Luck, 1x The Water Crystal

**Post-Board Gameplan:**
Survive the early onslaught with efficient removal, then stabilize with board wipes and lifegain. The mill plan is secondary — staying alive is the priority. Authority of the Consuls is devastating against aggro: their creatures enter tapped (no haste damage) and every creature they play gains you 1 life, fueling Hope Estheim's end-step mill. Bounce Off at 1 mana answers early threats before Day of Judgment mana is available — critically, it triggers Siege for mill 4 while buying time. Elspeth, Storm Slayer creates 2 tokens per +1 (her static doubles token creation), providing blockers while her -3 removes their biggest threat.

Negate comes out because aggro runs mostly creatures — countering a noncreature spell is unlikely to matter. Make Your Own Luck at 5 mana is too slow against a deck that kills on T4-5. The Water Crystal is cut because the 4-mana investment is better spent on Day of Judgment to survive. The mill engine doesn't need Crystal to win the long game against aggro — Siege + Hope Estheim is sufficient once stabilized.

**Key Cards:** Day of Judgment, Elspeth's Smite, Authority of the Consuls, Ancient Cornucopia

**Critical Turns:**
- **T1:** Deploy Authority of the Consuls if in hand. Otherwise, hold up Elspeth's Smite.
- **T2:** Hope Estheim if safe (lifelink blocks), or hold up No More Lies to counter a key 2-drop.
- **T3:** Glacierwood Siege if ahead on board, or trade with Get Lost + mill 4.
- **T4:** Day of Judgment is mandatory if they have 3+ creatures. This is the stabilization point.
- **T5+:** If alive at 10+ life with Siege, the game is won. Each removal spell mills 4 while answering their threats.

## vs. Midrange (Golgari, Jund, Abzan Value)

**Boarding:**
- **In (4):** 2x Disdainful Stroke, 2x Stroke of Midnight
- **Out (4):** 4x Elspeth's Smite

**Post-Board Gameplan:**
Midrange decks rely on individually powerful threats at 3-5 CMC. Disdainful Stroke efficiently counters their 4+ CMC bombs (Sheoldred, planeswalkers, Cavalier of Thorns) while triggering Siege for mill 4. Stroke of Midnight is unconditional instant-speed removal that hits any nonland permanent — planeswalkers, enchantments, and creatures alike — and triggers Siege. The 1/1 Human token it gives the opponent is irrelevant against our board wipes and mill plan.

Elspeth's Smite comes out because midrange creatures rarely attack recklessly into open mana, and their threats are often too large for 3 damage or have abilities that matter even without attacking. Get Lost handles anything that resolves, and Day of Judgment resets the board if they develop multiple threats.

The mill plan is excellent here. Midrange decks can't pressure fast enough to race the mill clock, and their value engines (graveyard recursion, card advantage) don't matter when their library is disappearing. Focus on T3 Siege → T4-5 interaction/wipes → T6-7 Space-Time Anomaly to close.

**Key Cards:** Glacierwood Siege, Disdainful Stroke, Get Lost, Day of Judgment

**Critical Turns:**
- **T3:** Siege is the absolute priority. Deploy it even without counter backup — midrange rarely has instant-speed enchantment removal.
- **T4:** Day of Judgment if they have 2+ threats, or Get Lost their best creature (mill 4).
- **T5:** Make Your Own Luck for double Siege triggers and card advantage.
- **T6-7:** Space-Time Anomaly should be lethal if they've been milled for 25+ cards already.

## vs. Control (Azorius, Dimir, Esper)

**Boarding:**
- **In (7):** 2x Negate, 2x Disdainful Stroke, 1x Long River's Pull, 1x Banishing Light, 1x Elspeth, Storm Slayer
- **Out (7):** 3x Day of Judgment, 4x Elspeth's Smite

**Post-Board Gameplan:**
Board wipes and creature removal are dead cards against control. Remove all 3 Day of Judgment and all 4 Elspeth's Smite — they have no targets. Bring in the full counter suite: post-board you have 2 Negate (main) + 2 Negate (board) + 3 No More Lies + 2 Disdainful Stroke + 1 Long River's Pull = 10 counters plus 3 Three Steps Ahead in counter mode. This is a counter war deck now.

Banishing Light provides a proactive answer to resolved threats (their planeswalkers, enchantments) that bypasses the stack. Elspeth, Storm Slayer creates an alternate win condition through combat — doubled tokens (+1 creates 2 soldiers) pressure opposing planeswalkers while generating a clock.

The mill plan is excellent against control because Glacierwood Siege is hard to answer (most control decks don't run mainboard enchantment removal), and every counter war triggers Siege mill. Deploy Siege with counter backup (ideally T4 Siege + hold up Negate). Let Three Steps Ahead copy Siege for overwhelming mill pressure. The key is patience — don't overextend into their counterspells. Resolve one Siege, protect it, and every subsequent spell mills them out.

**Key Cards:** Glacierwood Siege, Negate, Three Steps Ahead, Disdainful Stroke

**Critical Turns:**
- **T3-4:** Deploy Siege only when you can protect it. Ideally T4 Siege + Negate, or T3 if they're tapped out.
- **T5-6:** Counter wars over key spells generate massive mill with Siege. 3 spells exchanged = 12 cards milled with 1 Siege.
- **T7+:** Space-Time Anomaly as a finisher. At 20+ life, this mills 24+ cards with Siege trigger. If they counter it, that's another Siege trigger.

## vs. Combo (Lotus Field, Enchantress, Artifact Combo)

**Boarding:**
- **In (5):** 2x Negate, 2x Disdainful Stroke, 1x Banishing Light
- **Out (5):** 3x Day of Judgment, 2x Elspeth's Smite

**Post-Board Gameplan:**
Combo decks rely on resolving specific key pieces. The counter suite disrupts their game plan — post-board you have 4 Negate, 3 No More Lies, 2 Disdainful Stroke, and 3 Three Steps Ahead for 12 potential counter effects. Banishing Light exiles any combo piece that resolves. Day of Judgment and Elspeth's Smite are cut since combo decks run few creatures (or their creatures are combo pieces better answered by exile effects).

The mill clock pressures them to combo before their library runs out. Focus on identifying and countering their key combo piece while maintaining mill pressure through Siege triggers on counter spells. If they assemble part of their combo, Banishing Light or Get Lost can break it up. Space-Time Anomaly provides a fast clock — combo decks often don't gain life, so 20 cards + Crystal + Siege = 28 cards in one shot.

**Key Cards:** Negate, Disdainful Stroke, Glacierwood Siege, Get Lost, Banishing Light

**Critical Turns:**
- **T2-3:** Hold up Negate or No More Lies for their ramp or combo enabler. Deploy Siege only if they can't combo this turn.
- **T4-5:** Counter their first combo attempt. With Siege out, each counter mills 4-8 cards.
- **T6-7:** If their combo is disrupted, mill them out. If they're reassembling, keep counter magic available.

## vs. Tempo (Izzet Prowess, Simic Flash, Azorius Spirits)

**Boarding:**
- **In (3):** 2x Bounce Off, 1x Authority of the Consuls
- **Out (3):** 1x Make Your Own Luck, 1x Space-Time Anomaly, 1x The Water Crystal

**Post-Board Gameplan:**
Tempo decks deploy cheap efficient threats and protect them with counterspells while pressuring your life total. Authority of the Consuls is backbreaking — their creatures enter tapped (no haste or flash-block value) and you gain life for each one (fueling Hope Estheim mill). Bounce Off at 1 mana answers their early threats without walking into their 2-mana counters — they won't waste a counter on a 1-mana bounce spell.

Cut expensive sorceries they'll never let you resolve: Make Your Own Luck at 5 mana into open counter mana is suicidal, Space-Time Anomaly at 4 mana sorcery-speed is too vulnerable, and Water Crystal at 4 mana doesn't impact the board immediately. Keep Day of Judgment as a reset they can't efficiently counter at instant speed (they tap out for threats, you wipe). Keep Negate and No More Lies to contest their key spells.

The mill plan works because their small creatures make Elspeth's Smite excellent, and every removal spell triggers Siege. Grind them out with efficient 1-2 mana interaction that mills 4-8 per spell while their threats die.

**Key Cards:** Elspeth's Smite, Day of Judgment, Glacierwood Siege, Authority of the Consuls

**Critical Turns:**
- **T1:** Authority of the Consuls if in hand — this single card can win the matchup.
- **T2:** Hold up No More Lies or Negate for their key threat/counter.
- **T3:** Deploy Siege if they're tapped out, or trade removal for their creature.
- **T4:** Day of Judgment resets the board. They often overcommit to pressure you.
- **T5+:** Siege + cheap interaction grinds them out. Each Smite/Bounce Off mills 4-8 for 1 mana.

## vs. Mill (Mirror / Dimir Mill)

**Boarding:**
- **In (5):** 2x Negate, 2x Disdainful Stroke, 1x Elspeth, Storm Slayer
- **Out (5):** 3x Day of Judgment, 2x Elspeth's Smite

**Post-Board Gameplan:**
The mirror is a counter war over mill enchantments. Board wipes and creature removal are worthless — neither side runs enough creatures to matter. Bring in the full counter suite to protect our Siege and counter theirs.

Our lifegain gives us a structural edge over Dimir Mill: Ancient Cornucopia and Hope Estheim extend the game by maintaining a high life total (relevant for Space-Time Anomaly), and Haliya draws extra cards for free, improving our ability to find and protect Siege. Against opposing mill, our library is under attack too — faster mill rate wins. With 4 Glacierwood Siege, The Water Crystal (+4 per trigger), and Space-Time Anomaly (mill = life total), we have the highest raw mill output in the format.

Elspeth, Storm Slayer provides an alternate win condition through combat damage with doubled tokens — if the counter war is at a stalemate, deploying Elspeth forces them to answer a threat on a different axis. Disdainful Stroke catches their expensive mill spells and finishers.

**Key Cards:** Glacierwood Siege, Three Steps Ahead (copy Siege), Negate, The Water Crystal

**Critical Turns:**
- **T3:** Deploy Siege first if possible. Counter their Siege if they lead.
- **T4:** The Water Crystal accelerates your mill rate beyond theirs — each Siege trigger mills 8 instead of 4.
- **T5-6:** Three Steps Ahead to copy your Siege is often game-ending (mill 8-12 per spell).
- **T7:** Space-Time Anomaly at 20+ life = mill 24-28 cards in one shot. This is usually lethal.
