## vs. Aggro (Red Deck Wins, White Weenie, Boros)

**Boarding:**
- Out: 2x Negate, 1x Make Your Own Luck, 1x Omenpath Journey
- In: 2x Bounce Off, 1x Authority of the Consuls, 1x Elspeth, Storm Slayer

**Gameplan:** Survive the early onslaught, then stabilize with board wipes and lifegain. Authority of the Consuls slows their creatures and gains incremental life. Bounce Off answers early threats before wipe mana is available. Elspeth creates chump blockers and removes key threats. Negate is weak here since aggro runs mostly creatures. Post-board we have 5 board wipes, 3 Elspeth's Smite, and Authority to contain the swarm. Glacierwood Siege is less critical — winning through mill is secondary to not dying. Focus on wiping, gaining life with Fumigate/Cornucopia/Hope Estheim, and closing with any available win condition.

**Key Cards:** Day of Judgment, Fumigate, Elspeth's Smite, Authority of the Consuls

**Critical Turns:** Turn 1-2: Deploy Authority or hold up Smite. Turn 4: Day of Judgment must resolve. Turn 5: Fumigate for lifegain + wipe. If you stabilize at 10+ life with Siege on board, the game is usually won.

## vs. Midrange (Golgari, Jund, Abzan Value)

**Boarding:**
- Out: 3x Elspeth's Smite, 1x No More Lies
- In: 2x Rest in Peace, 2x Disdainful Stroke

**Gameplan:** Midrange decks rely on individual powerful threats and graveyard value. Rest in Peace shuts down their recursion and deathtouch-recursion engines. Disdainful Stroke counters their expensive threats (4+ CMC) efficiently. Elspeth's Smite comes out because midrange creatures rarely attack into open mana carelessly, and their threats are often too large for 3 damage. Instead, lean on Get Lost (exiles anything) and board wipes. Glacierwood Siege is excellent here — midrange can't pressure fast enough to stop the mill engine. Focus on deploying Siege Turn 3, wiping their board Turn 4-5, and milling them out while they rebuild.

**Key Cards:** Glacierwood Siege, Day of Judgment, Get Lost, Rest in Peace

**Critical Turns:** Turn 3: Siege is priority. Turn 5: Make Your Own Luck for card advantage and double mill triggers. If they overcommit post-wipe, a second Day of Judgment or Fumigate buries them.

## vs. Control (Azorius, Dimir, Esper)

**Boarding:**
- Out: 3x Day of Judgment, 2x Fumigate, 2x Elspeth's Smite
- In: 2x Negate, 2x Disdainful Stroke, 2x Heritage Reclamation, 1x Elspeth, Storm Slayer

**Gameplan:** Board wipes are dead cards against control. Trim all 5 wraths and Elspeth's Smite (no creatures to kill). Bring in the full counter suite — 4 Negate + 2 Disdainful Stroke + 2 No More Lies gives us 8 counters to fight over key spells. Heritage Reclamation answers their enchantments or artifacts. Elspeth, Storm Slayer provides a threat that generates tokens and can close games. The mill plan is strong here: Glacierwood Siege is hard for control to answer without specific enchantment removal, and every counter war triggers more mill. Deploy Siege with counter backup. Let Three Steps Ahead copy Siege for overwhelming mill pressure.

**Key Cards:** Glacierwood Siege, Negate, Three Steps Ahead, Disdainful Stroke

**Critical Turns:** Turn 3: Deploy Siege only with counter backup (ideally Turn 4 Siege + Negate). Counter wars on Turn 5-7 generate massive mill with Siege out. If they tap out for a planeswalker, Get Lost handles it while milling 4.

## vs. Combo (Ramp, Enchantress, Artifact Combo)

**Boarding:**
- Out: 3x Day of Judgment, 2x Elspeth's Smite
- In: 2x Negate, 2x Heritage Reclamation, 1x Break Down the Door

**Gameplan:** Combo decks rely on specific pieces. Counter magic disrupts their key spells, and Heritage Reclamation/Break Down the Door exile their enchantment or artifact combo pieces. Day of Judgment and Smite come out since combo decks run few creatures. The mill clock pressures them to combo before their library runs out. Focus on identifying and countering their key combo piece while maintaining mill pressure. Negate is excellent here since combo decks rely heavily on noncreature spells.

**Key Cards:** Negate, Heritage Reclamation, Glacierwood Siege, Three Steps Ahead

**Critical Turns:** Turn 2-3: Hold up Negate for their ramp spell or combo enabler. Turn 4-5: Deploy Siege if the coast is clear. Counter their first combo attempt, then mill them out while they search for another.

## vs. Tempo (Izzet Prowess, Simic Flash)

**Boarding:**
- Out: 1x Vaultborn Tyrant, 1x Omenpath Journey, 1x Ugin Eye of the Storms
- In: 2x Bounce Off, 1x Authority of the Consuls

**Gameplan:** Tempo decks try to deploy cheap threats and protect them with counters while pressuring your life total. Authority of the Consuls buys crucial time by making their creatures enter tapped (no haste value) and gaining incremental life. Bounce Off answers their early threats at minimal mana investment, avoiding their counter magic (they won't counter a 1-mana bounce). Cut expensive cards they'll never let you resolve (Vaultborn at 7, Ugin at 7, Omenpath at 4). Keep Day of Judgment as a reset button they can't efficiently counter. The mill plan is strong because their small creatures mean Elspeth's Smite is live, and every interaction spell mills 4 with Siege.

**Key Cards:** Elspeth's Smite, Day of Judgment, Glacierwood Siege, Authority of the Consuls

**Critical Turns:** Turn 1: Deploy Authority of the Consuls if in hand — it's backbreaking against tempo. Turn 2-3: Trade 1-for-1 with removal while developing mana. Turn 4: Day of Judgment resets the board. Turn 5+: Siege + interaction grinds them out.

## vs. Mill (Mirror / Dimir Mill)

**Boarding:**
- Out: 3x Day of Judgment, 2x Fumigate
- In: 2x Negate, 2x Disdainful Stroke, 1x Elspeth, Storm Slayer

**Gameplan:** The mirror is a counter war over mill enchantments. Whoever resolves and protects their mill engine wins. Our lifegain gives us an edge — Ancient Cornucopia and Hope Estheim extend the game by gaining life (irrelevant for mill but buys time). Board wipes are useless. Bring in all available counters to protect our Siege and counter theirs. Elspeth provides an alternate win condition through combat damage with tokens. The key insight: with 3 Glacierwood Siege + Three Steps Ahead copies, we can outpace opposing mill engines in raw mill speed.

**Key Cards:** Glacierwood Siege, Three Steps Ahead, Negate, Disdainful Stroke

**Critical Turns:** Turn 3: Whoever deploys Siege first has the advantage. Counter their Siege, resolve yours. Turn 5-6: Three Steps Ahead to copy your Siege is often game-ending.
