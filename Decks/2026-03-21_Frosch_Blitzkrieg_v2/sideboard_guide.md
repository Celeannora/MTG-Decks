# Sideboard Guide — Frosch Blitzkrieg v2

## Sideboard Composition

| Card | Count | Purpose |
|------|-------|---------|
| Disdainful Stroke | 3 | Counter CMC 4+ threats (ramp, control finishers, combo pieces) |
| Stickytongue Sentinel | 3 | Flash blocker + bounce own creature to dodge removal vs. aggro |
| Blossoming Defense | 2 | Hexproof protection vs. exile removal; +2/+2 pump |
| Three Steps Ahead | 2 | Counter spell OR copy key creature OR draw 2 (Spree flexibility) |
| Synchronized Charge | 2 | Distribute 2 counters + vigilance/trample vs. midrange brawls |
| Confounding Riddle | 2 | Counter CMC 4+ OR loot 4 for selection; two-mode flexibility |
| Dreamdew Entrancer | 1 | Second copy vs. matchups with must-answer single threats |

---

## Matchup Plans

### vs. Red Aggro / Mono-Red Burn

**Plan:** Deathtouch frogs trade up, Biosynthic Burst saves key pieces, Stickytongue Sentinel flashes in as surprise blockers, bounce engine stabilizes life total.

**Board Out:**
- −2 Glacierwood Siege *(too slow; you can't afford turn 3 tapped out against burn)*
- −1 Valley Floodcaller *(flash is irrelevant when you're on the back foot)*
- −1 Dreamdew Entrancer *(3 stun counters on their 2/2 is not where you want to be)*
- −1 Three Tree Scribe *(bounce-loop payoff; not relevant in a race)*

**Board In:**
- +3 Stickytongue Sentinel *(flash 2/1 blocker; can bounce your own creature to save it from burn)*
- +2 Blossoming Defense *(hexproof + +2/+2 protects Valley Mightcaller from a Shock mid-combat)*

**Key notes:** Frog Butler and Poison Dart Frog are your best cards here. A 2/2 deathtouch that taps for any color mana forces them to never attack profitably. Don't overextend into sweepers — keep Repulsive Mutation mana available to save your key creatures.

---

### vs. Blue Control (Azorius/Dimir)

**Plan:** Clement generates mana from Frogs → cast everything uncounterable effectively; Repulsive Mutation answers their answers; Three Steps Ahead copies key creatures.

**Board Out:**
- −2 Sunshower Druid *(life gain irrelevant; board presence is what matters)*
- −1 Three Tree Scribe *(bounce payoff not critical against control)*
- −1 Dreamdew Entrancer *(they don't have singular must-answer creatures usually)*

**Board In:**
- +2 Three Steps Ahead *(counter their counterspells {1}{U}; or copy Clement/Valley Mightcaller)*
- +2 Confounding Riddle *(counter their 4+ CMC finishers or loot for selection)*

**Key notes:** Repulsive Mutation is your best card in this matchup. Cast it for X=2 on Frog Butler (now a 4/2), then any spell they cast costs {4} or gets countered. Hold two mana every turn for Repulsive Mutation as a "soft counter." With Clement on board, your Frogs tap for {G}/{U} — you can represent a counter for basically free every turn.

---

### vs. Midrange (Golgari/Jund/Rakdos)

**Plan:** Repulsive Mutation counters their spot removal; Inspiring Call protects the board from sweepers; outrace on counter-pump advantage.

**Board Out:**
- −2 Sunshower Druid *(passive ETB gain doesn't contribute to the race here)*
- −2 Polliwallop *(they have large creatures; your deathtouch frogs handle that better)*

**Board In:**
- +2 Synchronized Charge *(distribute counters mid-combat + vigilance/trample is excellent in races)*
- +2 Confounding Riddle *(loot mode finds Repulsive Mutation or Inspiring Call; counter mode hits their 4-drops)*

**Key notes:** The midrange matchup is generally favored. You out-grind them on board through the bounce-and-counter-stack loop. Focus on getting Innkeeper's Talent to Level 2 (ward) — every Repulsive Mutation target becomes a 2-tax to remove. They'll run out of removal before you run out of Frogs.

---

### vs. Tokens / Wide Board (BW, GW, etc.)

**Plan:** Polliwallop as 1-mana creature kill once Frogs are out; Disdainful Stroke stops their anthems; Dreamdew Entrancer shuts down their best lord.

**Board Out:**
- −2 Inspiring Call *(effective, but less impactful when they're also going wide)*
- −1 Dour Port-Mage *(draw trigger not a priority)*
- −1 Valley Floodcaller *(flash untap less relevant vs. wide boards)*
- −1 Three Tree Scribe *(bounce loop secondary in fast matchup)*

**Board In:**
- +3 Disdainful Stroke *(hits Anthems, Lord spells, and their big finishers)*
- +2 Synchronized Charge *(trample through wide boards that can't block your large Frogs)*

**Key notes:** Valley Mightcaller's counter-stacking means your 1-drops become 5/5 and 6/6 tramples that go over their 1/1 tokens. Polliwallop deals double power damage — a 4-power Frog kills an 8-toughness creature. Target their lord or biggest blocker.

---

### vs. Ramp (Green Ramp / Domain)

**Plan:** Race with Blitzkrieg speed; Repulsive Mutation counters their big spells; Disdainful Stroke taxes their curve.

**Board Out:**
- −2 Sunshower Druid *(life gain is irrelevant in a race)*
- −1 Three Tree Scribe *(bounce payoff not needed)*
- −1 Dreamdew Entrancer *(3-turn stun is slow compared to just killing them)*
- −1 Dour Port-Mage *(draw engine not needed if we're racing)*

**Board In:**
- +3 Disdainful Stroke *(counters every ramp card CMC 4+; stops Atraxa, Sunfall, Finale of Devastation)*
- +2 Three Steps Ahead *(flexible — counter their finisher or copy Valley Mightcaller)*

**Key notes:** Repulsive Mutation is your primary interaction tool. A 5-power Frog with a {3}{G}{U} Repulsive Mutation threatens to counter anything they cast unless they pay 5. Against ramp, this is effectively a 1-mana "counter target spell unless they pay 5" after turn 4.

---

## General Sideboarding Principles

1. **Never board out all 4 Repulsive Mutations.** It is the defining interactive piece of v2.
2. **Innkeeper's Talent stays in every matchup.** Level 3 + Repulsive Mutation is the win condition.
3. **Inspiring Call comes out vs. aggro (too slow) but stays vs. everything else.** Drawing 4 cards and making your board indestructible on turn 5 wins most non-aggro games.
4. **Biosynthic Burst stays in all matchups.** Indestructible + untap for {1}{G} is always relevant.
5. **Clement (2x) is your most important piece against control.** Never side him out.
