## vs Izzet Prowess (17.2% of meta)

**Matchup Assessment:** Favorable (55/45 post-board)

**Their gameplan:** Deploy low-cost prowess threats (1-2 drops), chain spells for +1/+1 counters, win with 3/4+ trampling creatures on turn 4-5.

**Our gameplan:** 
- Authority of the Consuls taxes every creature they play (entering tapped = no attack)
- No More Lies + Disdainful Stroke protect Hope Estheim
- Build life total via lifelinkers; mill them out before they can overwhelm us

**Board In:**
```
+2 Abrade
+2 Authority of the Consuls (if not already at 4)
+2 Disdainful Stroke (if not already at 4)
```

**Board Out:**
```
-1 Lyra Dawnbringer (too slow; they go wide, not tall)
-1 The Water Crystal (too slow vs fast aggro)
-1 Resplendent Angel (needs setup time)
-1 Delney, Streetwise Lookout (doesn't help at instant speed vs prowess)
```

**Key notes:**
- Keep Authority of the Consuls hand if mulliganing — it's the single best card in this matchup
- Don't tap out for Space-Time Anomaly; keep No More Lies up to counter their spells
- Windcrag Siege tokens can chump-block and gain life on offense

## vs Mono-Green Landfall (14.0% of meta)

**Matchup Assessment:** Even (50/50 pre-board, 55/45 post-board)

**Their gameplan:** Ramp via landfall, play oversized threats (5/5+ creatures), overwhelm with combat damage by turn 5-6.

**Our gameplan:**
- Disdainful Stroke counters their 4+ CMC bombs
- Get Lost removes their key creatures
- Build life total quickly; mill them out before their bombs can close games

**Board In:**
```
+2 Disdainful Stroke (if not already at 4)
+2 Stroke of Midnight (answers 4/4+ tokens)
+1 Watcher of the Wayside (ETB mill slows their card advantage)
```

**Board Out:**
```
-1 Delney, Streetwise Lookout (their threats have power 3+; Delney's blocking protection irrelevant)
-2 Opt (lower priority draw vs their pressure)
-2 Windcrag Siege (1/1 lifelink tokens too small vs 4/4-5/5s)
```

**Key notes:**
- Prioritize resolving Hope Estheim before their landfall ramp accelerates
- Resplendent Angel 4/4 tokens are excellent blockers against Green's creatures
- They typically have low counterspell density — resolve our threats openly

## vs Dimir Excruciator/Mill (5.7% of meta)

**Matchup Assessment:** Unfavorable (40/60 pre-board, 50/50 post-board)

**Their gameplan:** Mill your library with Excruciator and mill synergies (Dimir has Pro Tour-winning mill lists); simultaneously develop threats in graveyard via Deceit/reanimation.

**Our gameplan:**
- Race them on mill — our Hope Estheim + The Water Crystal plan can outrace their mill if we stabilize early
- Kutzil's Flanker exiles graveyard to shut off reanimation targets
- Fly over with Angels for combat damage win if mill race is unfavorable

**Board In:**
```
+2 Kutzil's Flanker (graveyard exile + lifegain)
+1 The Water Crystal (amplifies our mill; if they mill us, we need to mill them faster)
+1 Space-Time Anomaly (3rd copy for burst finish)
+2 Stroke of Midnight (answers their creatures)
```

**Board Out:**
```
-2 Authority of the Consuls (they have low creature count in some builds)
-2 Windcrag Siege (doesn't accelerate clock vs mill)
-1 Opt (replace with higher-impact cards)
-1 Delney (less relevant when racing on mill)
```

**Key notes:**
- The Water Crystal's "+4 mill per trigger" dramatically accelerates our mill vs their 60-card library  
- Kutzil's Flanker: play it with the "exile graveyard" mode to remove their reanimation targets  
- Prioritize flying attackers — Dimir often lacks flyers, so aerial beatdown can close quickly  
- If they resolve Excruciator, prioritize countering their graveyard synergies, not the Excruciator itself

## vs Jeskai Control (2.2% of meta)

**Matchup Assessment:** Challenging Mirror Match (45/55 pre-board, 50/50 post-board)

**Their gameplan:** Control the board with counterspells and removal; win with value threats in late game. Likely running similar tools (Get Lost, No More Lies, Consult the Star Charts).

**Our gameplan:**
- Out-value them on lifegain; our ETB engines generate more life in less time
- The Water Crystal (legendary) gives us a mill advantage they may not have
- Resplendent Angel tokens make winning through combat feasible if counterspell war drags out

**Board In:**
```
+1 The Water Crystal (2nd copy for mill angle)
+1 Space-Time Anomaly (3rd copy — their life total is often low from shocklands)
+2 Stillness in Motion (mill their library passively — opponent can't counter an upkeep trigger)
+2 Disdainful Stroke (if not already at 4)
```

**Board Out:**
```
-2 Authority of the Consuls (they play few creatures)
-2 Windcrag Siege (slow vs control unless untouched)
-2 Get Lost (their wincons aren't often enchantments)
```

**Key notes:**
- Stillness in Motion is excellent in control mirrors — it mills 3 per upkeep passively; they can't use targeted counterspells on triggered abilities  
- Play Hope Estheim only when you have No More Lies open to protect it  
- Aerith Gainsborough's lifelink means combat-oriented Jeskai lists struggle to race our life total  
- Their life total is often 14-16 from shocklands — Space-Time Anomaly at that life total only mills them 14-16 cards; wait to cast it at 25+ life

## vs Mono-White Aggro / Momo (2.9% of meta)

**Matchup Assessment:** Favorable (60/40 pre-board, 65/35 post-board)

**Their gameplan:** Flood the board with small white creatures, gain incidental life themselves (making our mill win less reliable), win with wide attacks by turn 4-5.

**Our gameplan:**
- Authority of the Consuls taxes every creature they play; gains us as much life as they deploy
- Our lifelinkers outgain their incidental lifegain
- Dazzling Angel blocks profitably; Angel tokens trade up

**Board In:**
```
+2 Authority of the Consuls (if not already at 4)
+2 Stroke of Midnight (destroys their key pieces like Momo)
+2 Abrade (hits equipment/artifacts in Momo builds)
```

**Board Out:**
```
-1 The Water Crystal (too slow; they win before turn 4)
-2 Space-Time Anomaly (too slow; prioritize early stabilization)
-1 Delney (fragile vs their combat attacks)
-2 Disdainful Stroke (their spells are all low CMC)
```

**Key notes:**
- Prioritize getting Hinterland Sanctifier + Authority of the Consuls early — each of their creature plays gains us life  
- Hope Estheim at 2 mana blocks their 1-drops and grows in relevance each turn  
- Resplendent Angel's 4/4 token generation is backbreaking — they can't attack through a 4/4 flyer

## vs Mono-Red Aggro

**Matchup Assessment:** Favorable (65/35 pre-board, 70/30 post-board)

**Their gameplan:** Burn you out with direct damage spells and hasty creatures; win before you establish lifegain.

**Our gameplan:**
- Life total grows faster than they can burn — Authority of the Consuls + Hinterland Sanctifier + Hope Estheim lifelink recover health rapidly
- Windcrag Siege tokens chump-block hasty creatures while gaining life
- No More Lies counters their key burn spells on the stack

**Board In:**
```
+2 Authority of the Consuls (if not already at 4)
+2 Kutzil's Flanker (flash creature; gains 2 life, scries 2 — excellent recovery tool)
+2 Abrade (destroys their key artifacts like Embercleave effects)
```

**Board Out:**
```
-1 The Water Crystal (too slow vs burn)
-2 Space-Time Anomaly (4-mana sorcery is too slow vs pure burn)
-2 Disdainful Stroke (most burn spells are CMC 1-3)
-1 Delney (fragile at 3 toughness vs burn)
```

**Key notes:**
- Save No More Lies for their best burn spells (typically 3-5 damage), not early pings  
- Lyra Dawnbringer is game-ending against Mono-Red if she resolves — 5/5 first strike lifelink stops any attack profitably, and the angel pump + lifelink anthem ends the game within 2 turns  
- Windcrag Siege's lifelink Goblin token attacks for 1 lifelink every turn; over a game this recovers 4-6 life

## Boarding Philosophy

**The core tension**: This deck's mainboard is built to operate as a proactive control strategy. In reactive matchups (aggro), board in cards that slow the opponent while preserving lifegain. In proactive matchups (control, mirrors), board in additional mill finishers and passive engines they can't interact with easily.

**Never board out more than 2 of your lifegain creatures** — they are the engine that fuels Hope Estheim's win condition.

**Priority when boarding:**
1. Keep all Hope Estheim and lifegain 1-drops (non-negotiable)
2. Adjust counterspell package (more vs control, fewer vs aggro)  
3. Adjust removal (more vs creature decks, fewer vs control)
4. Add Stillness in Motion vs control for passive mill
5. Add Kutzil's Flanker vs graveyard strategies for graveyard hate
