# Sideboard Guide — Esper Hope

## Sideboard Composition

| Card | Count | Purpose |
|------|-------|---------|
| Moment of Craving | 2 | -2/-2 instant + gain 2 life; anti-aggro removal that triggers lifegain |
| Enduring Innocence | 2 | Lifelink enchantment creature; draws cards off small creatures entering |
| Bitter Triumph | 1 | 2-mana instant destroy creature/planeswalker; flexible removal |
| Negate | 2 | 2-mana hard counter for noncreature spells; anti-control |
| Enduring Curiosity | 2 | Flash 4/3; draws cards on combat damage to opponents; value engine |
| Spellgyre | 1 | 4-mana counter OR surveil 2 + draw 2; flexible dual mode |
| Angel of Finality | 2 | ETB exiles entire graveyard; flying Angel body for synergy |
| Malicious Eclipse | 2 | -2/-2 sweeper that exiles dying opponents' creatures; stops recursion |
| Consuming Ashes | 1 | 4-mana exile target creature; conditional surveil 2 |

## Matchup Plans

### vs. Red Aggro / Mono-Red Burn

**Plan:** Survive, gain life, stabilize behind lifegain creatures, win.

**Board Out:**
- −2 The Mindskinner *(too slow at {U}{U}{U}; they'll kill you before mill matters)*
- −1 Stillness in Motion *(passive mill too slow against fast aggro)*
- −2 Think Twice *(need proactive plays, not slow card draw)*

**Board In:**
- +2 Moment of Craving *(-2/-2 kills their 1-2 drops + gains 2 life for Hope triggers)*
- +2 Enduring Innocence *(lifelink enchantment creature; card draw off their small creatures)*
- +1 Bitter Triumph *(additional instant-speed removal for Phoenixes or hasty threats)*

**Key notes:** Authority of the Consuls on T1 is the best possible opener — their creatures entering tapped buys you full turns. Sheltered by Ghosts on Hope Estheim creates a protected lifelink threat they struggle to remove through ward {2}. Ajani's Pridemate grows fast as you stabilize.

---

### vs. Green Stompy / Gruul Ramp

**Plan:** Remove their key threats on curve, establish fliers they can't block, mill or fly over for the win.

**Board Out:**
- −2 The Mindskinner *(their trample creatures race mill; need removal instead)*
- −1 Spell Pierce *(less effective against creature-heavy decks)*

**Board In:**
- +2 Malicious Eclipse *(-2/-2 sweep clears their mana dorks and small creatures; exiles prevent recursion)*
- +1 Bitter Triumph *(additional unconditional creature removal)*

**Key notes:** Heartless Act and Get Lost handle their big threats efficiently. Lyra Dawnbringer as a 5/5 first strike lifelink blocks everything they have. Focus on establishing a flying board that goes over their ground attackers.

---

### vs. Blue Control (Azorius/Dimir/Esper Mirror)

**Plan:** Resolve threats through counterspell wars, mill as the primary win condition, grind with card advantage.

**Board Out:**
- −2 Heartless Act *(few creatures to target)*
- −2 Get Lost *(less useful without targets)*
- −1 Authority of the Consuls *(no creatures entering frequently on their side)*

**Board In:**
- +2 Negate *(hard counter for their counterspells, board wipes, and planeswalkers)*
- +2 Enduring Curiosity *(flash creature dodges sorcery-speed removal; draws cards on combat damage)*
- +1 Spellgyre *(flexible counter or card draw; excellent in counter wars)*

**Key notes:** The Mindskinner is your best card here — unblockable mill that doesn't care about their permission. Deploy it when they're tapped out or when you can protect it with Spell Pierce/No More Lies. Enduring Curiosity at flash speed on their end step presents a must-answer threat that replaces itself.

---

### vs. Graveyard / Reanimator Decks

**Plan:** Counter their key reanimation spells (No More Lies exiles), exile their graveyard with Angel of Finality, and race with the mill plan.

**Board Out:**
- −2 Ajani's Pridemate *(growth is slow and doesn't address the matchup's key axis)*
- −1 Authority of the Consuls *(lifegain is less relevant when they combo kill)*
- −2 Think Twice *(too slow; need interaction, not card draw)*

**Board In:**
- +2 Angel of Finality *(ETB exiles their entire graveyard; flying body contributes to the Angel plan)*
- +1 Consuming Ashes *(exiles their reanimated threat with conditional surveil)*
- +2 Malicious Eclipse *(exile clause prevents creatures from returning to graveyard)*

**Key notes:** No More Lies is your best tool — it exiles the countered spell rather than putting it in the graveyard. Keep Spell Pierce and Three Steps Ahead mana available at all times to counter reanimation spells. Angel of Finality timing is critical: deploy after they load the graveyard but before they reanimate.

---

### vs. Midrange (Golgari/Rakdos/Jund)

**Plan:** Out-value on lifegain, fly over their ground creatures, use Exemplar of Light to exile their key permanents.

**Board Out:**
- −1 Stillness in Motion *(mill is too passive against midrange value engines)*
- −1 Spell Pierce *(their threats are mostly creatures; less noncreature targets)*
- −1 Think Twice *(trim slow card draw for proactive plays)*

**Board In:**
- +2 Enduring Innocence *(additional lifelink creature + card draw engine)*
- +1 Bitter Triumph *(additional flexible removal for their planeswalkers and creatures)*

**Key notes:** This is typically your best matchup. They have individual card quality but can't match the combination of lifegain + mill + aerial pressure. Focus on establishing multiple Hope Estheim triggers per turn. Exemplar of Light removing their key permanents by spending growth counters is devastating.

---

## General Sideboarding Principles

1. **Never board out all 4 copies of Hope Estheim.** He is the deck's engine and reason to exist.
2. **The Mindskinner is the first card to board out against aggro.** Triple-blue on turn 3 is too slow when you're being burned out. Keep it against control and midrange.
3. **Sheltered by Ghosts stays in almost every matchup.** Removal + lifelink + ward is too versatile to cut.
4. **No More Lies is the backbone of the permission package.** It exiles what it counters, which matters against recursion decks.
5. **Malicious Eclipse is your best sideboard card against creature decks.** The exile clause prevents dying creatures from hitting the graveyard.
6. **Angel of Finality is a surgical tool.** Only bring it in against graveyard-dependent strategies; a 3/4 flier for {3}{W} is below rate otherwise.
