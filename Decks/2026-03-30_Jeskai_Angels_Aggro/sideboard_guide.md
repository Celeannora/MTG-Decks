## Matchup-Specific Plans

### vs. Jeskai/Azorius Control
**Expected cards from them:** Fumigate, Supreme Verdict, No More Lies, Temporary Lockdown, counterspells, planeswalkers

**In (6):**
- +3 No More Lies
- +2 Kutzil's Flanker
- +1 Enduring Innocence

**Out (6):**
- -2 Authority of the Consuls (they play few creatures)
- -1 Lightning Helix (less relevant vs non-creature threats)
- -2 Angel of Vitality (too slow if they wipe before 25 life)
- -1 Dazzling Angel (fragile 3-drop into sweepers)

**Plan:** Commit 2-3 threats max, hold up No More Lies for board wipes. Kutzil's Flanker prevents them from using instant-speed interaction on your turn — they can't Helix or counter your creatures when you're attacking. Enduring Innocence replaces wiped boards with card draw.

### vs. Izzet Prowess
**Expected cards from them:** Monastery Swiftspear, Slickshot Show-Off, Shock, Play with Fire, Monstrous Rage

**In (4):**
- +1 Authority of the Consuls (3rd copy — their creatures enter tapped)
- +2 Abrade (kills Swiftspear, Show-Off before pump)
- +1 Torch the Tower (cheap instant removal)

**Out (4):**
- -1 Delney (too slow at 3 mana in a race)
- -1 Resplendent Angel (too slow — game decided by T4)
- -2 Dazzling Angel (3/2 body trades poorly)

**Plan:** Authority of the Consuls is the MVP — their creatures enter tapped so they can't attack immediately after Prowess triggers. Race with lifelink while removing their key threats on sight. Sanctifier + Pridemate outgrows them by T3.

### vs. Mono-Green Landfall / Stompy
**Expected cards from them:** Territorial Scythecat, Coiling Stalker, Aftermath Analyst, large bodies

**In (3):**
- +2 Get Lost (exiles their biggest threat)
- +1 Torch the Tower (cheap removal for ramp creatures)

**Out (3):**
- -2 Authority of the Consuls (they don't flood creatures — quality over quantity)
- -1 Warleader's Call (slower than just attacking over them)

**Plan:** Fly over their ground blockers with Hawk, Essence Channeler, Angel of Vitality, Resplendent Angel. They have almost zero flying interaction. Get Lost handles anything that races you on the ground. Keep growing Pridemate — they can't block it profitably.

### vs. Dimir Mill

**In (5):**
- +3 No More Lies (counter their mill spells and removal)

### vs. Mono-White Aggro
**Expected cards from them:** Hopeful Initiate, Luminarch Aspirant, Thalia, Adeline, Wedding Announcement

**In (4):**
- +1 Authority of the Consuls (taps their creatures on entry)
- +1 Day of Judgment (emergency reset if behind on board)
- +2 Unwanted Remake (exile Adeline, Thalia, or Wedding Announcement)

**Out (4):**
- -1 Delney (both sides go wide — Delney too slow)
- -1 Resplendent Angel (T3 slot is crowded in mirror)
- -2 Anim Pakal (too slow to build up in aggro mirror)

**Plan:** This is a race. Authority of the Consuls gives you a massive tempo advantage — their creatures enter tapped while yours attack immediately. Lifelink creatures let you outrace their damage. Day of Judgment is the emergency button if they get too far ahead on board.

### vs. Mono-Red Burn
**Expected cards from them:** Shock, Play with Fire, Lightning Strike, Roiling Vortex, Eidolon of the Great Revel

**In (4):**
- +1 Authority of the Consuls (3rd copy for any creature-based builds)
- +2 Abrade (kills their creatures before they deal damage)
- +1 Torch the Tower

**Out (4):**
- -1 Delney
- -1 Resplendent Angel
- -2 Dazzling Angel

**Plan:** Lifegain is the entire plan. They can't outrace Healer's Hawk + Sanctifier + Pridemate gaining 4-5 life per turn while growing a 5/5. Angel of Vitality amplifies every lifegain trigger. Lightning Helix is both removal and a 6-life swing. You win the long game — just survive T3-4.

## General Sideboard Principles

1. **Never board out the core engine:** Sanctifier, Hawk, Pridemate, Channeler, Voice of Victory stay in every matchup. These are the kill condition.
2. **Authority of the Consuls scales with opponent creature count.** Max 3 copies vs creature-heavy aggro. Cut to 0 vs creatureless control.
3. **No More Lies is your only countermagic.** Board it in any time you expect board wipes, planeswalkers, or combo pieces.
4. **Day of Judgment is a last resort.** Only board in when you expect to be behind on board. You'll lose your creatures too — but Enduring Innocence and Channeler's counter-transfer soften the blow.
5. **Kutzil's Flanker is underrated.** Ward 2 during your turn means opponents can't efficiently use removal during combat. Forces them to act on their own turn, buying you a full attack step.
