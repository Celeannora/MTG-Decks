# Simic Ramp/Control - Sideboard Guide

## Sideboard Overview
```
2 Aether Spike - Counter wars, protect key spells
3 Disdainful Stroke - Counter expensive spells (4+ CMC)
2 Elspeth's Smite - Cheap removal for aggro
2 Tear Asunder - Exile problematic permanents
3 Struggle for Project Parity - Board wipe vs aggro
3 Nissa, Ascended Animist - Uncounterable threat vs control
```

---

## Matchup: Mono-Red Aggro
**Game 1 Win Rate**: 35% (UNFAVORABLE)  
**Post-Board Win Rate**: 60% (FAVORABLE)

### Sideboard Plan
**OUT** (5 cards):
- -3 Negate (they play few instants worth countering)
- -2 Confiscate (too slow, they kill us first)

**IN** (5 cards):
- +3 Struggle for Project Parity (board wipe + life gain)
- +2 Elspeth's Smite (cheap removal for attackers)

### Strategy Shift
- **Pre-board**: Try to survive with counters, deploy Overlord late
- **Post-board**: AGGRESSIVE mulligans for interaction. Accept card disadvantage to stay alive.
- **Key turns**: Turn 3-4 is make-or-break. If we survive to turn 5, we win.
- **Critical cards**: Struggle for Project Parity (wipes their board), Aftermath Analyst (blocks + ramps)

### Play Pattern
1. **Turn 1-2**: Take damage, play Llanowar Loamspeaker to block
2. **Turn 3**: Elspeth's Smite their best attacker OR Three Steps Ahead bounce
3. **Turn 4**: Struggle for Project Parity (wipe board, gain 5 life)
4. **Turn 5+**: They're in topdeck mode, we resolve any threat and win

### Key Decision Points
- **Mulligan aggressively**: Need 2-3 lands + removal/board wipe
- **Use life as a resource**: Going to 8 life is fine if we stabilize
- **Prioritize removal**: Don't try to counter their spells, kill their creatures
- **Save counters for burn**: Make Disappear their Lightning Strike, not their 2-drop

## Matchup: Azorius Control
**Game 1 Win Rate**: 45% (SLIGHTLY UNFAVORABLE)  
**Post-Board Win Rate**: 55% (SLIGHTLY FAVORABLE)

### Sideboard Plan
**OUT** (8 cards):
- -2 Sundering Eruption (they play few creatures)
- -3 Negate (too narrow vs their diverse threats)
- -3 Make Disappear (they can pay 2 mana easily)

**IN** (8 cards):
- +3 Nissa, Ascended Animist (UNCOUNTERABLE)
- +2 Aether Spike (counter their counter)
- +3 Disdainful Stroke (counter planeswalkers, Farewell, Sunfall)

### Strategy Shift
- **Pre-board**: Grind with value engines, hope to resolve one threat
- **Post-board**: DIVERSIFY threats. Force them to have answers for Nissa, Overlord, Kiora, Confiscate
- **Key turns**: Turn 6-8. This is when we deploy must-answer threats.
- **Critical cards**: Nissa (can't be countered), Aether Spike (protect our spells)

### Play Pattern
1. **Turn 1-5**: Ramp freely (they usually let early ramp resolve)
2. **Turn 6**: Nissa, Ascended Animist (UNCOUNTERABLE) - forces them to use removal
3. **Turn 7**: If Nissa resolved, create Phyrexian tokens. If not, try Overlord with Aether Spike backup
4. **Turn 8+**: Grind with multiple threats, steal their planeswalkers with Confiscate

### Key Decision Points
- **Don't overcommit**: They have Farewell/Sunfall. Play 1-2 threats at a time
- **Bait counters**: Try to resolve Kiora first, then slam Overlord
- **Use Nissa wisely**: Phyrexian mana tokens are LANDS (survive board wipes)
- **Counter their finishers**: Save Disdainful Stroke for The Eternal Wanderer, Teferi

## Matchup: Esper Midrange
**Game 1 Win Rate**: 50% (EVEN)  
**Post-Board Win Rate**: 55% (SLIGHTLY FAVORABLE)

### Sideboard Plan
**OUT** (5 cards):
- -2 Sundering Eruption (only decent vs go-wide)
- -3 Negate (they play more creatures than instants)

**IN** (5 cards):
- +3 Disdainful Stroke (counter Sheoldred, Liliana, Raffine)
- +2 Tear Asunder (exile Wedding Announcement, Sheoldred)

### Strategy Shift
- **Pre-board**: Race to deploy value engines before they land Sheoldred
- **Post-board**: EXILE their key cards. Don't let Sheoldred/Liliana resolve.
- **Key turns**: Turn 4-5. Whoever lands their 4-drop first usually wins.
- **Critical cards**: Disdainful Stroke (counter their bombs), Confiscate (steal Sheoldred)

### Play Pattern
1. **Turn 1-3**: Ramp with Loamspeaker/Analyst
2. **Turn 4**: Hold up Disdainful Stroke for Sheoldred
3. **Turn 5**: If they have no Sheoldred, deploy Extravagant Replication or Kiora
4. **Turn 6+**: Confiscate their Sheoldred/Liliana. If we steal Sheoldred, we win.

### Key Decision Points
- **Must counter Sheoldred**: This card draws them ahead and kills us
- **Confiscate priority**: Sheoldred > Liliana > Raffine > Wedding Announcement
- **Protect Extravagant Replication**: If they can't remove it, we bury them in value
- **Use Tear Asunder**: Exile Wedding Announcement before it flips

## Matchup: Domain Ramp
**Game 1 Win Rate**: 55% (SLIGHTLY FAVORABLE)  
**Post-Board Win Rate**: 50% (EVEN)

### Sideboard Plan
**OUT** (5 cards):
- -2 Make Disappear (they can pay casualty cost easily)
- -3 Negate (narrow vs their threats)

**IN** (5 cards):
- +3 Disdainful Stroke (counter Atraxa, Titan of Industry)
- +2 Aether Spike (protect our Overlord from counters)

### Strategy Shift
- **Pre-board**: Race to 6 mana for Overlord (we're slightly faster)
- **Post-board**: PROTECT Overlord. They want to counter it - use Aether Spike.
- **Key turns**: Turn 5-6. First player to resolve 6-drop usually wins.
- **Critical cards**: Overlord (must resolve), Confiscate (steal their Atraxa)

### Play Pattern
1. **Turn 1-4**: Ramp as fast as possible
2. **Turn 5**: If they tap out, deploy Extravagant Replication or hold up counter
3. **Turn 6**: Overlord with counter backup (Aether Spike)
4. **Turn 7+**: If Overlord resolved, we generate more mana. Create multiple Everywhere lands.

### Key Decision Points
- **Race to Overlord**: Turn 6 Overlord beats their turn 7 Atraxa
- **Don't tap out**: They might counter our key spell
- **Confiscate their bomb**: If they land Atraxa first, steal it immediately
- **Extravagant Replication on Overlord**: Creates exponential mana advantage

## Matchup: Orzhov Midrange
**Game 1 Win Rate**: 60% (FAVORABLE)  
**Post-Board Win Rate**: 65% (VERY FAVORABLE)

### Sideboard Plan
**OUT** (3 cards):
- -3 Negate (they're creature-heavy)

**IN** (3 cards):
- +2 Tear Asunder (exile Sheoldred, removal spells)
- +1 Elspeth's Smite (additional removal)

### Strategy Shift
- **Pre-board**: Grind with value engines, steal their Sheoldred
- **Post-board**: SAME PLAN but more removal
- **Key turns**: Turn 5-6. Confiscating their Sheoldred wins the game.
- **Critical cards**: Confiscate (steal Sheoldred), Three Steps Ahead (tempo plays)

### Play Pattern
1. **Turn 1-4**: Ramp safely, they can't pressure fast
2. **Turn 5**: Likely they play Sheoldred or Sanctuary Warden
3. **Turn 6**: Confiscate their best permanent (usually Sheoldred)
4. **Turn 7+**: If we have their Sheoldred, they lose to card advantage + life drain

### Key Decision Points
- **Confiscate priority**: Sheoldred > Sanctuary Warden > Elesh Norn
- **Counter their removal**: Save counters for their removal spells (Cut Down, Go for the Throat)
- **Don't overextend**: They have board wipes (Temporary Lockdown, Get Lost)
- **Extravagant Replication**: If we copy Sheoldred, we HARD WIN

## General Sideboarding Principles

### Against Aggro (Red, White Weenie, Gruul)
- **OUT**: Negate, Confiscate, slow cards
- **IN**: Struggle for Project Parity, Elspeth's Smite, early interaction
- **Goal**: Survive to turn 5, board wipe, win with any threat

### Against Control (Azorius, Dimir, Esper Control)
- **OUT**: Sundering Eruption, Make Disappear, creature-focused removal
- **IN**: Nissa, Aether Spike, Disdainful Stroke
- **Goal**: Resolve Nissa (uncounterable), diversify threats, protect key spells

### Against Midrange (Golgari, Orzhov, Esper Midrange)
- **OUT**: Negate (usually)
- **IN**: Disdainful Stroke, Tear Asunder
- **Goal**: Counter/remove their bombs, steal with Confiscate, outvalue

### Against Combo (Amalia Combo, Mill)
- **OUT**: Sundering Eruption, slow cards
- **IN**: All counters, Aether Spike
- **Goal**: Disrupt combo, race with Overlord

## Advanced Sideboarding Tips

1. **Don't over-sideboard**: Only swap 3-8 cards typically
2. **Keep your gameplan**: Don't sideboard out all your threats
3. **Test configurations**: Some matchups need experimentation
4. **Adjust to meta**: If lots of aggro, mainboard Struggle for Project Parity
5. **Know your outs**: Always have a plan to win post-board
